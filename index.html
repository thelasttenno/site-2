The Mental Benefits of Biking and The Negative Rep That Pirate Builders Get

I have been an avid mountain biker and dirt jumper since I was 10 or 11 and have been building and maintaining jumps and trails since I was 13 or 14. it opens up a whole new world and set of challenges for me. Riding is an outlet; it is something I can channel pent up energy through. And it is something that no matter how defeated I am feeling in life I can go and do that has some form of progression whether it is riding skills or visible trail beatification/work, that I can bring back to my daily life and help me stay energized. Mountain biking has a rich community effect, and large physical and mental health benefits, as well as fostering an understanding of the importance of the land and treating it properly.

“I will say mountain biking improves my mental health. It helps me burn off stress, decreases depression and anxiety, reduces irritability, and just helps me clean out my head and all that “junk” that gets stored up. I’m able to get a lot of things straightened out in my head while I ride. I also use the time spent riding just meditating and marveling at nature and all of God’s creation which helps me build spiritually and gain a greater connection… and that’s the largest part of my mental health improvement.” 

Mountain biking is a great analogy for life. If you hold on too hard you will have an unpleasant and rough ride. Don’t hold on hard enough and you will fall off. But when you hold on just right everything flows smoothly.

“Mountain biking is a great analogy for life. If you hold on too hard you will have an unpleasant and rough ride. Don’t hold on hard enough and you will fall off. But when you hold on just right everything flows smoothly. Ultimately you are really not in control, in this case, gravity is mostly in control. You are just Appling influence to increase the surface area of your success. Concern yourself too much with the future and what might go wrong, you will manifest that destiny. Think too much about your last fail and you won’t be able to enjoy the present. Ride the middle and flow from one obstacle to the next and only give attention to where you want to go.”

no dig no ride

With all the fun and health benefits that mountain biking can offer, there is also controversy surrounding it. Critics believe that bikers are negatively influencing the environment. I believe it’s not who is using the trails, but how the trails are built and maintained. Properly built and maintained trails are the key to treading lightly on the land. A huge rule of riding is no dig no ride and if mountain bikers see a problem while on the trails, they take the time to stop and repair the trail for other riders. We also set aside days as mountain bike groups to remove obstacles on trails that may cause trail users to detour off the path and maintain jumps to combat erosion and misuse from RC cars and younger kids who run on the lips and landings. And from my experience as a Pirate bush builder and a legitimate trail builder for my town's skills park I can say that the pirate jumps are cleaner, better kept, and 99% of the time on crown land or land where the property owners have allowed it for decades.  

To protect the environment as you bike, follow the International Mountain Bicycling Association's (IMBA) Rules of the Trail:

1. Ride only on open trails.

2. Leave no trace.

3. Control your bicycle.

4. Yield to others.

5. Never scare animals.

6. Plan ahead.

These basic rules will not only help protect the environment but will also help to build strong relationships among hikers, bikers, equestrians, and other trail users [source: IMBA].
